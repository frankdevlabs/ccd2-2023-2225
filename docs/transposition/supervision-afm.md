# Supervision — AFM, and the BNPL market context

> The conduct supervisor's role and the market the new rules address.

## AFM as supervisor

- **AFM** (Autoriteit Financiële Markten) is the conduct supervisor for consumer credit. CCDII page:
  https://www.afm.nl/nl-nl/sector/kredietaanbieders/4-herziene-richtlijn-consumentenkrediet-ccdii
- Ahead of the act's adoption, AFM has decided to **accept licence applications** from credit providers so
  assessments can be done in time before **20 Nov 2026** — applicants bear the cost/risk, and licences
  **cannot be granted before the rules are finalised**.
- AFM is a non-voting member of the European supervisory college for Klarna.
- New supervisory tooling in the bill: **product-intervention powers** (art 1:77q Wft).

## AFM CCDII supervisory expectations (sector page)

The AFM's CCDII sector page sets out what it expects of credit providers under the recast rules:

- **Creditworthiness** stays an **open norm** — AFM expects further specification via sector
  methodology (e.g. the **VFN** code) and says it is discussing the expected standard with the market.
- **Age verification** must use a **reliable source** ("betrouwbare bron") such as **iDIN**,
  operationalising the ban on credit to minors.
- **Early signalling** ("vroegtijdige signalering") of payment problems and referral to debt
  assistance; AFM will update its **"Consumenten en Incassotrajecten"** guidance.
- Previously exempt agreements (incl. "uitstel van betaling" / BNPL) come under **Wft** conduct
  supervision, bringing new providers and intermediaries into scope.

## BNPL market (AFM Marktupdate 2025, June 2025)

- In 2024 the four providers (**Billink, in3, Klarna, Riverty**) processed "**53 miljoen
  BNPL-transacties… goed voor €5,1 miljard**" (up ~17%).
- AFM supports lowering the **€250 BKR threshold** because "9 op de 10 transacties met betalingsproblemen
  een waarde van onder de €250 hebben"; the current BKR check "geldt voor kredieten hoger dan €250 met een
  looptijd van langer dan één maand."
- "**1 op de 6** [BNPL-gebruikers onder de 35] staat op minstens 90 dagen per jaar rood."
- The three e-commerce platforms (Amazon, Bol, Zalando) had 4.4 million Dutch deferred-payment users in
  2024 vs ~5.5 million for the four BNPL providers.
- AFM supports rapid **statutory age verification**.
- Report (PDF): https://www.afm.nl/~/profmedia/files/rapporten/2025/rapport-marktupdate--bnpl-2025-ned.pdf

## BNPL self-regulation (interim)

Pending the act's application the cabinet relies on sector self-regulation via the **Gedragscode BNPL**.
AFM names four NL signatory providers: **Billink, in3, Klarna and Riverty** (noting "Tinka, which is no
longer active"). Government statement (31 Oct 2025):
https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/actueel/nieuws/2025/10/31/nieuwe-regels-voor-buy-now-pay-later
