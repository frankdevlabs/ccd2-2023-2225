# Summary — what this file does

CCD2 — **Directive (EU) 2023/2225** — is the EU's revised consumer-credit rulebook, replacing the 2008
directive (CCD1) from 20 November 2026. For the Netherlands it is implemented by the **Implementatiewet
herziene richtlijn consumentenkrediet** (Kamerstuk **36924**) plus an **Implementatiebesluit** (AMvB),
amending mainly the Wet op het financieel toezicht (Wft) and Boek 7 BW. In plain terms:

1. **More credit comes under the rules.** BNPL ("buy now, pay later"), credit cards and authorised
   overdrafts are brought into scope; the old €200 lower limit is abolished and the ceiling rises to
   €100,000. BNPL providers now generally need an **AFM licence**. A narrow exception remains for
   genuinely short (≤50-day), cost-free deferral by the supplier itself.

2. **Lending must be assessed more carefully.** The creditworthiness test is recast as "necessary and
   proportionate," and lenders are **banned** from using social-network data or special-category personal
   data (health, religion, etc.). BKR must be consulted for **every** consumer-credit agreement (the €250
   threshold is removed).

3. **No credit to minors.** A public-law ban plus mandatory age verification (new Wft 4:34b); agreements
   with minors become **voidable** (BW 7:75(4)).

4. **Cleaner advertising.** New prohibitions — e.g. against suggesting that credit improves your finances,
   or that a BKR registration barely matters.

5. **Help when things go wrong.** Lenders must detect payment problems early and refer consumers in
   difficulty to debt-advice services (Wft 4:35b / BW 7:68a).

Where it stands: NL **missed** the 20 Nov 2025 transposition deadline and the Commission opened
infringement proceedings (letter of formal notice, 30 Jan 2026); the bill is in Tweede Kamer written
preparation; the target is entry into force on **20 Nov 2026** (deferred-debit cards 6 months later).
