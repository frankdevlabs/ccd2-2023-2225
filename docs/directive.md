## Identification

- **Full title:** Directive (EU) 2023/2225 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 18 October
  2023 on credit agreements for consumers and repealing Directive 2008/48/EC.
- **Short name:** Consumer Credit Directive II (CCD2 / CCDII).
- **CELEX:** 32023L2225 · **ELI:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2023/2225/oj
- **Official Journal:** OJ L, 2023/2225, 30 October 2023.
- **Legal base:** Article 114 TFEU. Largely **maximum harmonisation** (Article 42 lists the regulatory
  choices / Member-State options).

## Scope & thresholds

- CCD1 covered agreements of **€200–€75,000**. CCD2 **abolishes the €200 floor** and raises the ceiling
  to **€100,000** (Art 2(2)(c)); certain provisions still apply above €100,000 for unsecured
  residential-renovation credit (Art 2(3)).
- Brings **BNPL, credit cards and authorised overdrafts** into scope.

## Staggered / transitional provisions

- **Open-end agreements existing on 20 Nov 2026:** transitional regime under Art 47 (certain CCD2
  articles apply — arts 23, 24, 25(1) 2nd sentence, 25(2), 28, 39; otherwise CCD1 continues until
  termination).
- **Deferred-debit cards:** a Member-State option to exempt. The Netherlands does **not** exempt them but
  applies the rules **6 months later** (new Wft 1:20(4)) — see [provision](provisions/deferred-debit-cards.md).
- **Penalties / competent authorities:** competent authorities notified by 20 Nov 2025; penalty rules by
  20 Nov 2026.
- **APR / cost caps:** Member States report measures by 20 Nov 2026; EBA report by 20 Nov 2029.

## Status of EU-level transposition

The Netherlands and 22 other Member States missed the 20 Nov 2025 deadline and received a Commission
letter of formal notice on 30 Jan 2026 — see [`infringement.md`](infringement.md). Denmark and Malta
transposed on time. The Dutch transposition is tracked in [`transposition/`](transposition/).
