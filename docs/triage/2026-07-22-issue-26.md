## What was flagged

The daily tracker saw the normalized-body hash change on **both** AFM CCDII guidance pages in the same
day (re-fetched twice — stable, so a real content change, not chrome rotation). Titles unchanged;
in-scope. It could not diff against a prior snapshot, so it flagged for manual review of *what*
specifically was updated. Precedent: #11 (same two pages).

Both pages fetched fresh from the live site (curl-impersonate tier, HTTP 200) and read against the June
2026 text already captured in the repo (issue #11). The genuinely **new** substance:

**T1-07 (overview page)** — now names the specific Wft licensing hooks and adds an intermediary route:
- **Art 2:60 Wft** vergunningplicht for providers of deferred-payment services ("buy now, pay later"),
  deferred-debit cards, and hire/lease with an option to buy.
- **Art 2:81 Wft** registratieplicht for **large intermediaries** in short-term BNPL credit (jointly with
  the providers of that credit).
- **Art 2:80 Wft** vergunningplicht for intermediaries in newly-supervised credit forms that fall under no
  exception/exemption/registration duty.
- AFM will let credit providers **register their deferred-payment intermediaries**; process, conditions and
  costs to follow "later this year".

**T1-11 (drie doorlopende verplichtingen)** — the proportionele-invulling section is materially expanded:
- **BKR arrears → refuse credit:** "Als uit het BKR een bestaande achterstand blijkt, wordt geen krediet
  verstrekt."
- The financial-position assessment **may not rest solely on the consumer's credit history**, and **all
  information gathered must be verified** (e.g. income/assets).
- **"Race to the bottom":** AFM wants the sector to make joint acceptance-policy agreements; lending that
  deviates from those agreements can be an *indication* of irresponsible lending.
- The current **BNPL gedragscode** consumer-protection measures are expected to be retained — consulting the
  central **curatele-/bewindregister** and **blocking service on arrears**.
