### Why the original "verification" missed it (root cause)

The original triage queried `Document?$filter=Kamerstukdossier/any(k:k/Nummer eq 36924)` and saw max
Volgnummer 7, no nota — and concluded the nota did not exist. **That query is structurally blind to
freshly-published documents.** A newly-published TK document gets `Volgnummer = -1` and an **empty
`Kamerstukdossier` link** until the dossier metadata is assigned later, so the dossier-link filter omits
it. Querying the same OData feed by subject returns the nota immediately:

```
Document?$filter=year(Datum) eq 2026 and contains(tolower(Onderwerp),'consumentenkrediet')&$orderby=Datum desc
  -1  Nota n.a.v. het … verslag  "36924 Nota naar aanleiding van het verslag …"  2026-06-23  (2026D31985)
   7  Brief regering             "Reactie op verzoek commissie …"                 2026-06-23  (2026D31983)
  -1  Bijlage                    "Beslisnota bij 36924 Nota naar aanleiding …"    2026-06-23  (2026D31986)
  …
DocumentNummer eq '2026D31985' → Soort "Nota n.a.v. het … verslag", Datum 2026-06-23 (exists)
```

On the dossier **detail** page these recent documents are collapsed behind an extra click, so they are
also easy to miss there; the **search page** lists them directly:
`https://www.tweedekamer.nl/zoeken?qry=36924&fld_tk_categorie=Kamerstukken&srt=date%3Adesc%3Adate`.

So both the brief regering (36924-7) **and** the nota (36924-8) landed on 23 Jun 2026 — they are two
distinct documents, not a conflation.
