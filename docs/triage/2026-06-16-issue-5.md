## What was flagged
The VFN "Update Herziene richtlijn Consumentenkrediet (CCD II)" page changed (stable across two
fetches). The page (dated **2 maart 2026**) is a VFN analysis of the **Raad van State advies** on the
CCD II implementation bill. Confirmed by fetching the live page (browser UA; WebFetch is 403-blocked):

- RvS gave a **positief dictum B** — the bill can be submitted to the Tweede Kamer, but adjustment on
  several points is desirable. VFN flags two main RvS observations.
- **Kredietverstrekking aan minderjarigen.** The bill adds national elements on top of the directive
  (ban on uitstel van betaling / BNPL to minors; tightened consent requirement for other credit by
  minors; lender age-verification duty). RvS: it is insufficiently substantiated on which CCD II
  grondslag these national restrictions rest, and a *categorale uitsluiting* of minors fits poorly within
  the individual-creditworthiness-test system the directive prescribes; legally stronger to position age
  as an **access condition** ("toegangsvoorwaarde tot krediet"), if objectively justified.
- **Verwerking van bijzondere categorieën persoonsgegevens.** CCD II bars special-category data (e.g.
  religion, health) from the creditworthiness assessment. The bill requires such data be "**afgescheiden**"
  via technical/organisational measures. VFN states this point "is voor de VFN van groot belang geweest"
  and that it lobbied via roundtables, presentations and position papers. RvS finds the proposed statutory
  text does not align well with the goal stated in the MvT, considers the current formulation *possibly in
  conflict with CCD II*, and notes the AVG (GDPR) always applies — so clarification/amendment looks needed.
- **Planning.** MinFin is processing the advice; aims to submit the bill to the TK by **end of March**,
  for treatment before the summer recess; the AMvB goes to RvS only after the TK handles the bill. Full
  implementation target **20 Nov 2026**, "likely under pressure," esp. from BNPL motions in the TK.
