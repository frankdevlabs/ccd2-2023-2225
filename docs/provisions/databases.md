# Databases (credit registration)

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Art 19** —
> [`creditworthiness-database.md#article-19`](../../extracts/directive/creditworthiness-database.md#article-19).
> **Dutch implementation:** **Wft 4:32** ([`#wft-4-32`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-I-wft.md#wft-4-32))
> + AMvB BKR thresholds ([`#bgfo-creditworthiness`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/amvb_implementatiebesluit.md#bgfo-creditworthiness)).

## What CCD2 requires
- **Non-discriminatory cross-border access** for creditors to databases used to assess creditworthiness;
  only supervised, GDPR-compliant creditors may access them (Art 19(1)–(3)).
- Databases must hold at least **arrears, type of credit and creditor identity** (Art 19(4)); creditors
  must not process special-category or social-network data from them (Art 19(5)).
- On a database-based rejection the consumer must be **informed without delay and free of charge** of the
  result and the database consulted (Art 19(6)); consumers must be told of an arrears registration
  **within 30 days** (Art 19(7)) and have a complaints route (Art 19(8)).

## How the Netherlands implements it
- **Wft 4:32** is amended: the creditor must inform the consumer **within 30 days** of an arrears
  registration in a *stelsel van kredietregistratie* (BKR) and of the GDPR rights and the **categories of
  data taken into account** on rejection.
- The **AMvB** removes the **€250 BKR-consultation threshold** so BKR is consulted for every agreement
  (see [creditworthiness](creditworthiness.md)).
- The wider *stelsel van kredietregistratie* reform sits in the **adjacent Wetsvoorstel kredietregistratie
  (WGK025409)** — an out-of-scope file, see [`tracker.yaml`](../../tracker.yaml) `keep_apart`.
