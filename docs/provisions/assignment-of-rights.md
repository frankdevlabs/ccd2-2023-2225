# Assignment of rights

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Art 39** —
> [`forbearance-intermediaries-authorities.md#article-39`](../../extracts/directive/forbearance-intermediaries-authorities.md#article-39).
> **Dutch implementation:** existing **BW Boek 7** consumer-credit + Boek 6 cessie rules
> ([`36924_artikel-II-bw7.md`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-II-bw7.md)).

## What CCD2 requires
- On assignment of the creditor's rights (or the agreement) to a third party, the consumer may **plead
  against the assignee any defence available against the original creditor**, including set-off where
  permitted (Art 39(1)).
- The original creditor must **inform the consumer of the assignment**, unless it keeps servicing the
  credit by agreement with the assignee (Art 39(2)).

## How the Netherlands implements it
- Delivered through the **existing BW Boek 7 consumer-credit provisions** and the general Boek 6 rules on
  cessie/verweermiddelen, which already let the consumer raise defences against an assignee; bill 36924
  makes only conforming references.
- NL detail here is light — confirm against the MvT (36924-3) and the existing BW articles.

## Sourcing
