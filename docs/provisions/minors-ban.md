# Ban on credit to minors

> **Directive:** CCD2 art 6 — [`general-provisions.md#article-6`](../../extracts/directive/general-provisions.md#article-6).
> **Dutch implementation:** Wft **4:34b** ([`#wft-4-34b`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-I-wft.md#wft-4-34b))
> + BW **7:75 lid 4** ([`#bw-7-75`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-II-bw7.md#bw-7-75)).

## How the Netherlands implements it
- **New art 4:34b Wft** introduces a **public-law ban** on offering deferred-payment credit (incl. BNPL)
  to minors regardless of guardian consent, with **mandatory age verification**.
- Civilly flanked by **art 7:75(4) BW**, making such agreements **voidable ("vernietigbaar")**.
- For other (non-BNPL) credit, minors require legal-representative consent.

## Legal-basis change after RvS
After Raad van State criticism, the legal basis was changed from **CCD2 art 18(3)** (individual
creditworthiness) to **CCD2 art 6** (objective-justified age differentiation). — see the RvS line in
[`../transposition/nl-pipeline.md`](../transposition/nl-pipeline.md).
