# Conduct-of-business obligations

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Art 32** —
> [`apr-conduct.md#article-32`](../../extracts/directive/apr-conduct.md#article-32).
> **Dutch implementation:** the general Wft conduct-of-business / zorgplicht regime + BGfo remuneration
> rules ([`36924_artikel-I-wft.md`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-I-wft.md)).

## What CCD2 requires
- Creditors and intermediaries must act **honestly, fairly, transparently and professionally** and take
  account of consumers' rights and interests across the product lifecycle — manufacturing, advertising,
  granting, advising, ancillary services and execution (Art 32(1)).
- **Remuneration** of staff must not impede that duty; for staff assessing creditworthiness, remuneration
  must not be contingent on the number/proportion of approved applications, and advisory-staff pay must not
  be tied to sales targets (Art 32(2)–(4)). Member States may restrict pre-contract payments (Art 32(5)).

## How the Netherlands implements it
- Largely **pre-existing**: the Wft **algemene zorgplicht** (4:24a), the *ken-uw-klant* duties and the
  **BGfo beloningsregels / provisieregels** already deliver Art 32; bill 36924 fits the new credit types
  into that framework rather than creating a standalone article.
- NL detail here is lighter than the headline provisions — expand from the MvT (36924-3) as needed.
