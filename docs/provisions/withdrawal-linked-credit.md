# Right of withdrawal & linked credit agreements

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Arts 26–27** —
> [`overdraft-withdrawal-repayment.md#article-26`](../../extracts/directive/overdraft-withdrawal-repayment.md#article-26).
> **Dutch implementation:** **BW 7:66** ([`#bw-7-66`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-II-bw7.md#bw-7-66)).

## What CCD2 requires
- **Art 26** — a **14-calendar-day** right of withdrawal, running from conclusion or from receipt of the
  Art 20/21 terms; where those terms or the withdrawal information were not given, the period extends to
  **12 months and 14 days**. On withdrawal the consumer repays capital + accrued interest within 30 days;
  the creditor may claim no other compensation. A linked-purchase return policy longer than 14 days extends
  the period.
- **Art 27** — on withdrawal from the supply contract the consumer is **no longer bound by the linked
  credit agreement**, and may pursue remedies against the creditor where the supplier fails.

## How the Netherlands implements it
- **BW 7:66** (ontbinding/herroeping) gains a new lid 2: the period **expires 12 months + 14 days** after
  conclusion where Art 61 terms were not provided, and **does not run at all** where the consumer was not
  informed of the withdrawal period (61 lid 2 onderdeel q); new lid 9 extends withdrawal to match a
  **return policy longer than 14 days** for linked agreements.
- The 14-day baseline and linked-credit consequences (Art 27) sit in the existing BW Boek 7 afdeling
  consumentenkrediet.

## Sourcing
Voorstel van wet + MvT (36924-2/3).
