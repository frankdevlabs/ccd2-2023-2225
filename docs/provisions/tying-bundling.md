# Tying & bundling; inferred agreement

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Arts 14–15** —
> [`tying-advisory-unsolicited.md#article-14`](../../extracts/directive/tying-advisory-unsolicited.md#article-14).
> **Dutch implementation:** new **BW 7:60a / 7:60b** ([`#bw-7-60a`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-II-bw7.md#bw-7-60a))
> + the affirmative-consent rule in **BW 7:58a** ([`#bw-7-58a`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-II-bw7.md#bw-7-58a)).

## What CCD2 requires
- **Bundling allowed, tying prohibited** (Art 14(1)). A creditor may still require a payment/savings
  account, but only for the four listed purposes (repay/service the credit, pool resources, additional
  security) (Art 14(2)).
- Where insurance is required, the creditor must **accept an equivalent policy from another supplier**
  (Art 14(3)) and give the consumer **at least three days** to compare insurance offers (Art 14(5)).
- **Oncological data** may not be used for credit-linked insurance after a Member-State-set period of at
  most **15 years** from the end of treatment (Art 14(4)).
- **No inferred agreement** through default options / pre-ticked boxes; consent needs an unambiguous
  affirmative act (Art 15).

## How the Netherlands implements it
- **New BW 7:60a** transposes Art 14: koppelverkoop verboden, gebundelde verkoop toegestaan; the
  payment/savings-account carve-out; equivalent-insurance acceptance; the three-day insurance comparison
  window.
- **New BW 7:60b** sets the oncological-data limit at a Member-State-chosen **10 years** after full
  remission (and in any case 15 years after treatment ends).
- **BW 7:58a(2)–(3)** require express consent and bar consent inferred from pre-ticked default options.
