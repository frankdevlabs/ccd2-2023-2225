# Financial education

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Art 34** —
> [`forbearance-intermediaries-authorities.md#article-34`](../../extracts/directive/forbearance-intermediaries-authorities.md#article-34).
> **Dutch implementation:** largely **non-legislative** — existing financial-education policy (Wijzer in
> geldzaken and related initiatives), not a discrete provision in bill 36924.

## What CCD2 requires
- Member States shall **promote measures supporting consumer education** on responsible borrowing and debt
  management, with clear general information on the credit-granting process — especially for first-time and
  digital-channel borrowers — consulting consumer organisations (Art 34(1)).
- The Commission reports on financial education available across Member States and identifies best practice
  (Art 34(2)).

## How the Netherlands implements it
- Met mainly through **existing policy and stakeholder initiatives** (e.g. Wijzer in geldzaken, Nibud,
  AFM consumer guidance) rather than a new statutory article; the MvT treats Art 34 as a promotional duty.
- NL detail here is light by nature — confirm the government's stated approach in the MvT (36924-3).
