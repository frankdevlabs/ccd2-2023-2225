# Ban on unsolicited granting of credit

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Art 17** —
> [`tying-advisory-unsolicited.md#article-17`](../../extracts/directive/tying-advisory-unsolicited.md#article-17).
> **Dutch implementation:** **BW 7:58a(1)** ([`#bw-7-58a`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-II-bw7.md#bw-7-58a))
> + a borging duty in **Wft 4:34c** ([`#wft-4-34c`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-I-wft.md#wft-4-34c)).

## What CCD2 requires
- Member States shall **prohibit any granting of credit without the consumer's prior request and explicit
  agreement** (Art 17). This targets inertia selling / unsolicited credit lines and limit increases.

## How the Netherlands implements it
- **New BW 7:58a(1):** a credit agreement is concluded **only at the consumer's request**; the consumer is
  bound only by his express consent (7:58a(2)).
- **New Wft 4:34c:** a creditor must have **adequate processes to ensure compliance with BW 7:58a** —
  bringing the private-law prohibition within AFM conduct supervision.

## Sourcing
Voorstel van wet + MvT (36924-2/3).
