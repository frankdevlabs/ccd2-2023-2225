# Caps on borrowing rate, APR & total cost of credit

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Art 31** —
> [`apr-conduct.md#article-31`](../../extracts/directive/apr-conduct.md#article-31).
> **Dutch implementation:** the **maximale kredietvergoeding** in the Besluit kredietvergoeding
> ([`#besluit-kredietvergoeding`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/amvb_implementatiebesluit.md#besluit-kredietvergoeding))
> + the cost-charging rule in **BW 7:76** ([`#bw-7-76`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-II-bw7.md#bw-7-76)).

## What CCD2 requires
- Member States must **introduce measures to prevent abuse and excessively high** borrowing rates, APRs or
  total costs of credit — **"such as caps"** (Art 31(1)). This is mandatory; CCD1 left rate caps entirely
  to national discretion.
- Member States **may** prohibit or limit specific charges or fees (Art 31(2)).
- Member States **report their measures to the Commission by 20 Nov 2026**; the Commission publishes them
  by 20 Nov 2027; the EBA reports on implementation (including cap methodologies) by **20 Nov 2029**
  (Art 31(3)–(4)).

## How the Netherlands implements it
- The Netherlands **already operates a cap** — the **maximale kredietvergoeding** (currently 14 % nominal,
  the temporarily-lowered crisis level having been reviewed) set in the **Besluit kredietvergoeding** under
  BW 7:76 — so Art 31(1) is largely **pre-existing law** rather than a new obligation.
- **BW 7:76** is tidied: the creditor and supplier may not stipulate or charge any **kredietvergoeding**
  other than as permitted under leden 2–3; the former lid 4 lapses. The rate level itself stays at the
  AMvB level (Besluit kredietvergoeding) and is reported to the Commission.

## Sourcing
MvT (36924-3) + draft Implementatiebesluit. The exact cap percentage and any change on transposition must
be verified against the final Besluit kredietvergoeding / Staatsblad — flagged because the rate level is
politically live.
