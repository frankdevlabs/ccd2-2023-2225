# Harmonisation level, penalties & transitional regime

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Arts 42–47** —
> [`final-provisions.md#article-42`](../../extracts/directive/final-provisions.md#article-42).
> **Dutch implementation:** penalty annexes **Wft 1:79 / 1:80**
> ([`#wft-1-79`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-I-wft.md#wft-1-79)), the transitional law
> **Overgangswet NBW art 211a** ([`#ow-211a`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-IV-overgangswet.md#ow-211a))
> and the entry-into-force article ([`#inwerkingtreding`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_slotbepalingen.md#inwerkingtreding)).

## What CCD2 requires
- **Art 42 — maximum harmonisation:** Member States may not keep or introduce diverging provisions where
  CCD2 contains harmonised rules (subject to the Art 42 list of options).
- **Art 43** — the directive's protection is **imperative** (no contracting-out to the consumer's
  detriment). **Art 44** — penalties must be **effective, proportionate and dissuasive**.
- **Art 47 — repeal & transition:** CCD1 (2008/48/EC) is repealed from **20 Nov 2026**; existing open-end
  agreements move to a transitional regime (certain CCD2 articles apply). **Art 48** sets transposition
  (20 Nov 2025) and application (20 Nov 2026).

## How the Netherlands implements it
- **Wft 1:79 / 1:80** extend the AFM enforcement annexes (last onder dwangsom / bestuurlijke boete) to the
  new provisions — the Art 44 penalty step.
- **Overgangswet NBW (new art 211a)** sets the transitional regime for agreements existing at entry into
  force, mirroring Art 47.
- **ARTIKEL V (inwerkingtreding):** entry into force aligned to **20 Nov 2026**, with the **+6-month**
  switch-on for deferred-debit cards; **ARTIKEL VI** sets the citeertitel.
