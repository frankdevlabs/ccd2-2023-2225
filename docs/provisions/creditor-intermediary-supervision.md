# Admission, registration & supervision of creditors and intermediaries

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Arts 37–38** —
> [`forbearance-intermediaries-authorities.md#article-37`](../../extracts/directive/forbearance-intermediaries-authorities.md#article-37).
> **Dutch implementation:** **Wft 2:64c** ([`#wft-2-64c`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-I-wft.md#wft-2-64c))
> + **Wft 2:81** ([`#wft-2-81`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-I-wft.md#wft-2-81)).

## What CCD2 requires
- **Art 37** — creditors and intermediaries (other than credit/payment/e-money institutions) must be
  subject to an **adequate admission process, registration and supervision** by an independent competent
  authority. Member States may exempt SME suppliers acting as **ancillary** intermediaries or offering
  interest-free deferred payment with only limited late charges.
- **Art 38** — credit intermediaries must disclose the **extent of their powers** and whether they work
  for one/several creditors or independently, disclose and agree any consumer fees, and communicate those
  fees to the creditor for the APR.

## How the Netherlands implements it
- The newly in-scope providers (BNPL / cards / overdrafts) need an **AFM licence (Wft 2:60)** or
  intermediary registration (2:80); **Wft 2:64c** is amended on the admission conditions and **Wft 2:81**
  carries the lighter regime / ancillary-supplier treatment.
- Intermediary disclosure duties (Art 38) sit in the existing Wft 4:72–4:73 conduct regime.
