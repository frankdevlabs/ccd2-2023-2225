# Scope expansion — BNPL, credit cards, authorised overdrafts

> **Directive:** CCD2 arts 2, 4. **Dutch implementation:** Wft 1:1, 1:20, 2:60/2:80/2:81. Operative text
> (when transcribed): [`extracts/directive/scope-art2.md`](../../extracts/directive/scope-art2.md) ·
> [`extracts/transposition-nl/36924_scope-art2.md`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_scope-art2.md).

## What CCD2 requires
- Abolishes the CCD1 **€200 lower limit**; raises the ceiling to **€100,000** (art 2(2)(c)); some
  provisions still apply above €100,000 for unsecured residential-renovation credit (art 2(3)).
- Brings **BNPL, credit cards and authorised overdrafts** into scope.

## How the Netherlands implements it
- **"Verlenen van uitstel van betaling"** is added to the definition of credit (art 1:1 Wft), and the
  exceptions in art 1:20 Wft are removed — notably the exception for credit repayable within three months
  with only insignificant costs, used by BNPL.
- BNPL providers, deferred-debit-card issuers and authorised-overdraft ("rood staan") providers thus fall
  under the Wft and need an **AFM licence** (art 2:60 Wft) or, for intermediaries, art 2:80 Wft.
- A **narrow BNPL exception** remains in a new art 1:20 Wft where (i) the supplier itself grants the
  deferral (no third party, unless a platform), (ii) no interest/costs except limited late-payment costs,
  and (iii) full payment within **50 days** (14 days for large enterprises).
- MKB intermediaries with credit-broking as an ancillary activity are exempt; large suppliers brokering
  short-term BNPL face a lighter **registration** regime (art 2:81 Wft).
