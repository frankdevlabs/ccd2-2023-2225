# Advisory services

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Art 16** —
> [`tying-advisory-unsolicited.md#article-16`](../../extracts/directive/tying-advisory-unsolicited.md#article-16).
> **Dutch implementation:** **Wft 4:23** ([`#wft-4-23`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-I-wft.md#wft-4-23))
> on top of the existing advies / passendheid regime.

## What CCD2 requires
- The creditor/intermediary must **disclose whether advisory services are provided** and, beforehand, the
  basis of the recommendation (own range vs market) and any fee (Art 16(1)–(2)).
- Where advice is given: obtain the consumer's financial situation, **recommend suitable agreements**,
  act in the consumer's best interest, and give a **record of the recommendation** (Art 16(3)).
- Member States may restrict the terms "advice"/"advisor" and impose conditions on "independent advice"
  (Art 16(4)); must require risk warnings (Art 16(5)); advice may in principle only be provided by
  creditors/intermediaries (Art 16(6)).

## How the Netherlands implements it
- **Wft 4:23** is extended: when advising on credit (other than mortgage credit) the adviser must give the
  consumer a **copy of the advice on a durable medium**. The broader suitability/"ken-uw-klant" duties
  already sit in Wft 4:23–4:24.
