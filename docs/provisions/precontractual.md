# Pre-contractual information, explanations & personalised offers

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Arts 9–13** (general information, SECCI pre-contractual information, adequate
> explanations, personalised offers) —
> [`precontractual-information.md#article-10`](../../extracts/directive/precontractual-information.md#article-10).
> **Dutch implementation:** **BW 7:60** ([`#bw-7-60`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-II-bw7.md#bw-7-60)).
>
> Advertising (arts 7–8) is on its own page ([advertising](advertising.md)); withdrawal and early
> repayment (arts 26, 29) moved to [withdrawal & linked credit](withdrawal-linked-credit.md) and
> [open-end & early repayment](openend-early-repayment.md).

## What CCD2 requires
- **Art 9** — general information must be permanently available; **Art 10** — **SECCI-style**
  pre-contractual information in good time before the consumer is bound, with a reminder where it is given
  less than one day ahead.
- **Art 12** — **adequate explanations** so the consumer can assess whether the agreement suits their
  needs and the consequences of default.
- **Art 13** — where a **personalised offer is based on automated processing**, the consumer must be
  clearly informed.

## How the Netherlands implements it
- **BW 7:60** is recast to carry the whole chapter: clear, comparable pre-contractual information per
  Art 10 (lid 1); the **one-to-seven-day withdrawal reminder** where information is late (lid 2); the
  **adequate-explanation** duty with the prescribed elements (lid 3); the **automated-processing
  disclosure** for personalised offers (lid 4); a tailored regime for effectenkrediet (lid 5); and a
  carve-out for small/short interest-free credit (lid 6). Breach is an **oneerlijke handelspraktijk**
  (lid 7).
