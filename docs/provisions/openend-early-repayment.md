# Open-end agreements & early repayment

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Arts 28–29** —
> [`overdraft-withdrawal-repayment.md#article-28`](../../extracts/directive/overdraft-withdrawal-repayment.md#article-28).
> **Dutch implementation:** **BW 7:68** ([`#bw-7-68`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-II-bw7.md#bw-7-68)).

## What CCD2 requires
- **Art 28 (open-end credit):** the consumer may terminate **free of charge at any time** (notice ≤ 1
  month); the creditor may terminate on ≥ 2 months' notice, or suspend draw-downs for objectively
  justified reasons with notice.
- **Art 29 (early repayment):** the consumer may repay early at any time with a **reduction of the total
  cost of credit**; creditor compensation is capped at **1 %** (or 0.5 % if < 1 year remaining), never
  exceeding the interest that would have been paid; no compensation for overdrafts or variable-rate
  periods.

## How the Netherlands implements it
- **BW 7:68** (vervroegde aflossing): adds that **all costs charged to the consumer are taken into account**
  when calculating the cost reduction; raises the compensation threshold reference from **€75,000 to
  €100,000** (matching the new scope ceiling) and lets the consumer claim a reduction where the
  compensation claimed exceeds the actual loss.
- The open-end termination rules of Art 28 sit in the existing BW Boek 7 consumentenkrediet provisions; NL
  detail here is lighter — expand from the MvT (36924-3) as needed.

## Sourcing
Voorstel van wet + MvT (36924-2/3).
