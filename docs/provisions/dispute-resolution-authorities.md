# Out-of-court dispute resolution & competent authorities

> **Directive:** CCD2 **Arts 40–41** —
> [`forbearance-intermediaries-authorities.md#article-40`](../../extracts/directive/forbearance-intermediaries-authorities.md#article-40).
> **Dutch implementation:** AFM as competent authority + the new product-intervention power **Wft 1:77q**
> and the penalty annexes **Wft 1:79 / 1:80** ([`#wft-1-79`](../../extracts/transposition-nl/36924_artikel-I-wft.md#wft-1-79)).

## What CCD2 requires
- **Art 40** — consumers must have access to **adequate, prompt out-of-court dispute resolution**, using
  existing ADR entities meeting Directive 2013/11/EU, with cooperation on cross-border disputes.
- **Art 41** — Member States designate **independent competent authorities** with investigation and
  enforcement powers (not themselves creditors), bound by professional secrecy; **may grant product-
  intervention powers** to withdraw credit products in justified cases (Art 41(9)); notify the Commission
  by 20 Nov 2025.

## How the Netherlands implements it
- Out-of-court resolution runs through **Kifid** (the financial-services ombudsman); **AFM** is the
  designated competent authority with DNB for prudential aspects.
- **New Wft 1:77q** gives a **product-intervention power** (Art 41(9)); **Wft 1:79 / 1:80** add the new
  articles (4:34a–c, 4:35b, 1:77q) to the **last-onder-dwangsom / bestuurlijke-boete** annexes, bringing
  them within AFM enforcement.
