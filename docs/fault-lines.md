# Fault lines — the contested issues

> Where the Dutch implementation is contested or under pressure. Links down to [`provisions/`](provisions/)
> and [`stakeholders.md`](stakeholders.md).

- **Timing pressure to 20 Nov 2026.** Both the draft Implementatiebesluit and the sector acknowledge
  delay; full entry into force by the application date is the target but "under pressure." VFN: "Door
  vertraging in het nationale wetgevingsproces komt de voorbereidingstijd voor aanbieders steeds verder
  onder druk te staan." The missed deadline already drew a Commission [letter of formal
  notice](infringement.md). — [pipeline](transposition/nl-pipeline.md)

- **Scope of the narrow BNPL exception.** The ≤50-day, cost-free supplier-deferral carve-out (new Wft
  1:20) defines who escapes licensing. Its exact perimeter (third-party vs platform; large-enterprise
  14-day variant; intermediary regimes 2:80/2:81) is drawn largely from law-firm briefings and should be
  verified against the final text. — [scope](provisions/scope-bnpl-cards-overdraft.md)

- **Removing the €250 BKR threshold.** AFM backs it ("9 in 10 problem transactions are under €250");
  it raises BKR-consultation volume and cost for high-frequency BNPL. — [creditworthiness](provisions/creditworthiness.md)

- **Statutory age verification & the minors ban.** Strongly supported by AFM; after RvS criticism the
  legal basis shifted from CCD2 art 18(3) to art 6. Practical age-verification burden is the live
  question. — [minors ban](provisions/minors-ban.md)

- **Late-payment cost cap for BNPL/cards.** The new art 12 Besluit kredietvergoeding regime (max fee per
  notice/year) is contested by parts of the sector. Joint Betaalvereniging/NVB response targets the
  deferred-debit-card treatment. — [AMvB](transposition/implementatiebesluit.md)

- **Deferred-debit cards' 6-month delay.** Applying card rules 6 months after the rest (Wft 1:20(4)) is a
  pragmatic concession that the BvN/NVB response engages with. — [deferred-debit cards](provisions/deferred-debit-cards.md)
